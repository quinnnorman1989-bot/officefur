How to Choose the Right Standing Desk

1. Height Range Matters More Than You Think

If you're under 5'4" or over 6'2", double-check the height range carefully. Many desks don't go low enough for shorter users or high enough for tall users. The Autonomous SmartDesk Pro's 26"–52" range covers almost everyone.

2. Single Motor vs. Dual Motor

Dual motor desks are faster, quieter, and handle more weight. Unless you're on an extremely tight budget, always go dual motor. The difference in daily use is significant.

3. Don't Skip the Desktop Quality

Many budget desks sell frames without desktops. The desktop is what you look at all day — don't cheap out. Bamboo and solid wood options from Autonomous and Uplift are worth the upgrade.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are standing desks actually good for you?
Research shows that alternating between sitting and standing throughout the day reduces back pain, improves energy, and can help with weight management. The key is not standing all day — aim to stand 30–45 minutes per hour.
How much should I spend on a standing desk?
For a quality desk that will last 5+ years, budget at least $400–$500. The FlexiSpot E7 at $379 is the floor for quality. Below that, build quality and motor reliability drop significantly.
How long does assembly take?
Most standing desks take 1–2 hours to assemble with basic tools. We recommend having a second person help with attaching the desktop — it makes the process much easier.
