21:9 vs 32:9 — Which Aspect Ratio?

21:9 Ultrawide (Most Popular)

The 34" 21:9 format is the sweet spot for most home office workers. It's wide enough for two apps side by side, fits most desks, and has the most software support. This is what we recommend for 90% of people.

32:9 Super-Ultrawide

The 49" super-ultrawide (like the Samsung CRG9) truly replaces two monitors. It's incredible for trading, video editing, or data work. But it requires a very wide desk and powerful GPU — not for everyone.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will an ultrawide work with my MacBook?
Yes — most modern ultrawides connect via USB-C or Thunderbolt. The LG 34WP65C-B and LG 40WP95C-W both work seamlessly with MacBook Pro and Air via a single USB-C cable for display and charging.
Is ultrawide worth it over two monitors?
For most work tasks, yes. One ultrawide eliminates the distracting bezel gap between two monitors, uses fewer desk ports, and is much cleaner to manage. The only reason to choose two monitors is if you need one to be vertical (portrait mode).
Do I need a special desk for a 34" ultrawide?
A standard 48" wide desk works fine for a 34" ultrawide. For the 40" or 49" options, you'll want at least a 55"–60" desk. Check the physical width (not just the screen diagonal) before buying.
