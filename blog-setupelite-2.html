If you work from home, your chair is the single most important piece of equipment you own. The average remote worker sits for 8–10 hours a day. A bad chair costs you in back pain, lost focus, and medical bills. A great chair pays for itself within months.

We spent 6 weeks sitting in 20+ ergonomic chairs — testing adjustability, lumbar support quality, seat comfort during long sessions, and build quality. Here's the definitive ranking.

💡 Key insight: The biggest mistake people make is buying a chair based on looks. Ergonomics requires adjustability — the more settings a chair has, the more likely it will fit your specific body. Don't compromise on lumbar support.

What to Look for in an Ergonomic Chair

Lumbar Support is Non-Negotiable

Lower back pain is the #1 complaint among desk workers. A chair with adjustable lumbar support — ideally both in height and depth — is the most important feature. Fixed lumbar pads are mostly useless since they're designed for an average body that doesn't exist.

Seat Depth Adjustability

Most people skip this one and regret it. If the seat is too deep, you'll sit with poor posture or perch on the edge. The Branch and Autonomous ErgoChair both include this adjustment — most chairs at this price don't.

4D Armrests vs. Basic

4D armrests adjust in four directions: up/down, forward/back, left/right tilt, and pivot. This lets you position your arms perfectly to eliminate shoulder tension. It sounds minor — it isn't.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Herman Miller Aeron worth the $1,400 price tag?
If you work 8+ hours a day from home, yes. Amortized over 10 years (and it will last that long), it's about $140/year — less than most monthly gym memberships. The back pain it prevents will save you far more than that in physiotherapy costs.
Can a good chair fix my existing back pain?
A good ergonomic chair can significantly reduce and prevent back pain, but it's not a substitute for medical advice. If you have existing pain, see a physiotherapist and use a good chair as part of your recovery plan.
What size Aeron should I get?
Size A fits people under 5'4" and under 130 lbs. Size B (the most popular) fits most people 5'3"–6'0". Size C fits taller or larger people. When in doubt, go to a Herman Miller showroom and sit in all three.
